Promotion of Women and Girl Education

Project Name: Ms.Pragathi

Location: Bangalore rural and surrounding

Period: 3 to 6 Months

Implementing Partner: Mantarlee Social Innovations Federation – section 8 company

Support Amount: 11.35 lakhs

Project Brief

According to UNESCO, approximately 130 million girls remain out of school globally, facing barriers such as poverty, cultural norms, and early marriage.

In India, girls make up 58.65% of the total population, but their literacy rate is only 65.5%, compared to 82.1% for boys. This is often due to the pressure on girls to manage household chores and care for families, limiting their educational opportunities.

The Bala Pragathi program addresses these issues through focused efforts in:

  • Economic Growth: Educated women contribute significantly to economic development. Each additional year of schooling can increase a woman’s income by up to 20%.
  • Improved Health Outcomes: Education improves health awareness. Educated women are more likely to seek healthcare, reducing maternal and child mortality rates.
  • Social Development: Educated girls are more empowered to advocate for their rights and engage in decision-making processes, promoting gender equality and social justice.
  • Breaking the Cycle of Poverty: Education helps girls overcome poverty, providing better opportunities for future generations and reducing income inequality.

Key objectives

  1. To create awareness on gender equality.
  2. Awareness to face real life challenges including cyber security.
  3. Education empowers girls to break free from the cycle of poverty.
  4. Healthcare awareness program.
  5. Improve HDI & SDG index.

Deliverable/Outcome

  1. Infra: Smart Class , Computers & Printers & Touch TV / Projector.
  2. Library: Digital & Physical.
  3. Health care: Napkin Burning Machine.
  4. RO water Unit.
  5. Health Awareness Campaign by Doctors.

Qualitative Impact

  1. Educated girls leads to gender equality and social justice & free from poverty.
  2. Educating one girl child can 2.2 times more likely to be HIV +, 50% more likely to immunizer their children.
  3. 2 times more likely to educate their children, 14% reduction in child marriage. Each additional year of schooling can increase a woman’s income by up to 20%.

Quantitative Impact

  1. 500+ girl students will be trained on Health care , gender equality, importance of education.
  2. 30+ staffs from 2 schools will be trained on Sanity inside school / class rooms, Digital infra & other machines supplied to respective schools.
