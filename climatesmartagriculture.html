Climate Smart Agriculture

Project Name: Climate Smart Agriculture

Location: Pan India

Period: 1 to 3 years

Implementing Partner: Mantarlee Social Innovations Federation

Support Amount: 5 to 10 lakhs

Project Brief

The growing population and changes in diet are driving up the demand for food. According to the World Bank 2020 report, 8.9% of the population is hungry. The food security challenge will be more difficult as by 2050, we will need 70% more food. The challenge is intensified by agriculture's vulnerability to climate change. On farms, climate change is reducing crop yields, nutritional quality, and livestock productivity. There is a need for substantial investments in adaptation to maintain or improve yield and food quality. Climate Smart Agriculture (CSA) is an integrated approach to managing cropland, livestock, forests, and fisheries to address the interlinked challenges of food security and climate change. CSA aims to achieve three outcomes: Increased Productivity, Enhanced Resilience, and Reduced Emissions.

We have the infrastructure facility to train farmers in climate-smart best practices such as:

  • Improving yield by vermi composting
  • Using Bee Boxes
  • Applying active charcoal to enhance soil quality
  • Promoting agroforestry to increase farmer income

We are now focused on leveraging this facility to enable farmers to implement best practices through the provision of:

  • Vermi composting kits
  • Active charcoal production units
  • Bee Boxes
  • Native fruit plants like Lemon, Tamarind, and Coconut

Key objectives

  1. To create awareness on climate smart agriculture.
  2. To promote climate smart agriculture by improving infrastructure available to the farmers.
  3. Environment conservation by tree plantation in Agroforestry and their maintenance.

Deliverable/Outcome

  1. 200 farmers trained on climate smart agriculture.
  2. Installation and training to 30 farmers for vermi composting, Bee Box and active charcoal production.
  3. Agroforestry plantation for 30 farmers, 25 saplings each.

Qualitative Impact

This will be a very effective model of training, empowering and enabling farmers to implement climate smart agriculture and at the same time improving their livelihood and adding our bit to environment sustainability.

Quantitative Impact

1 facility leveraged and sustained 200 farmers trained on climate smart agriculture. 30 farmers trained, empowered and enabled to implement vermi composting, Bee Box, active charcoal production and agroforestry with 25 fruit plants.
