Promotion of Govt School Enrolment in Rural Areas

Project Name: Bala Pragathi

Location: Bangalore rural and surrounding

Period: 3 to 6 Months

Implementing Partner: Mantarlee Social Innovations Federation – section 8 company

Support Amount: 7 lakhs to 15 Lakhs

Project Brief

According to UNESCO, around 130 million girls are out of school globally, primarily due to poverty, cultural norms, and early marriage. Although girls make up 58.65% of the population, their literacy rate is only 65.5%, compared to 82.1% for boys. This gap is often driven by family responsibilities and household chores that hinder girls' education.

Educating girls leads to multiple long-term benefits including:

  • Economic Growth: Every additional year of schooling can raise a woman’s income by up to 20%.
  • Improved Health: Educated women are more aware of health practices, leading to lower maternal and child mortality.
  • Social Development: Education empowers girls to advocate for their rights and participate in community decision-making.
  • Poverty Reduction: Education helps break the cycle of poverty and reduce income inequality for future generations.

Key objectives

  1. To provide clean drinking water.
  2. To provide learning kits where learning becomes easy.
  3. Increase the physical and mental health of students.
  4. Education empowers next generation to break free from the cycle of poverty.
  5. Improve HDI & SDG index.

Deliverable/Outcome

  1. RO water Unit.
  2. Play Area Upgradation.
  3. School Infra for Pre-Primary school ( Desk / Bench / Civil works )

Qualitative Impact

  1. Better school infrastructure will bring more enrolment in Govt Primary School.
  2. Better enrolment improves social justice & first step to eradicate hunger & poverty.
  3. 10-15 % increase in school attendance due to clean drinking water.
  4. 10% increase in academic performance due to safe play area.

Quantitative Impact

  1. 200 to 400+ primary school students and 30 to 60 staff will be using clean drinking RO water.
  2. 75 to 150+ primary school students will be using Safe Play area to increase physical development, social interaction and motor skills.
  3. 75 to 150+ primary school students and 30 to 60 + staffs will be using upgraded school infra.
