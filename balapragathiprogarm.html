1, Promotion of Govt School Enrolment in Rural Areas

Project Name: Bala Pragathi

Location: Bangalore rural and surrounding

Period: 3 to 6 Months

Implementing Partner: Mantarlee Social Innovations Federation – section 8 company

Support Amount: 7 lakhs to 15 Lakhs

Project Brief

According to UNESCO, around 130 million girls are out of school globally, primarily due to poverty, cultural norms, and early marriage. Although girls make up 58.65% of the population, their literacy rate is only 65.5%, compared to 82.1% for boys. This gap is often driven by family responsibilities and household chores that hinder girls' education.

Educating girls leads to multiple long-term benefits including:

  • Economic Growth: Every additional year of schooling can raise a woman’s income by up to 20%.
  • Improved Health: Educated women are more aware of health practices, leading to lower maternal and child mortality.
  • Social Development: Education empowers girls to advocate for their rights and participate in community decision-making.
  • Poverty Reduction: Education helps break the cycle of poverty and reduce income inequality for future generations.

Key objectives

  1. To provide clean drinking water.
  2. To provide learning kits where learning becomes easy.
  3. Increase the physical and mental health of students.
  4. Education empowers next generation to break free from the cycle of poverty.
  5. Improve HDI & SDG index.

Deliverable/Outcome

  1. RO water Unit.
  2. Play Area Upgradation.
  3. School Infra for Pre-Primary school ( Desk / Bench / Civil works )

Qualitative Impact

  1. Better school infrastructure will bring more enrolment in Govt Primary School.
  2. Better enrolment improves social justice & first step to eradicate hunger & poverty.
  3. 10-15 % increase in school attendance due to clean drinking water.
  4. 10% increase in academic performance due to safe play area.

Quantitative Impact

  1. 200 to 400+ primary school students and 30 to 60 staff will be using clean drinking RO water.
  2. 75 to 150+ primary school students will be using Safe Play area to increase physical development, social interaction and motor skills.
  3. 75 to 150+ primary school students and 30 to 60 + staffs will be using upgraded school infra.

2, Promoting Hygiene & Sanitation

Project Name: Promoting Hygiene & Sanitation among School students

Location: Bangalore Urban and Rural

Period: 3 to 6 Months

Implementing Partner:Mantarlee Social Innovations Federation

Support Amount: Rs 17,70,000/-

Project Brief

Access to clean and functional sanitation facilities is crucial for the health and well-being of students. This program focuses on renovating and upgrading toilet facilities in schools to improve hygiene, accessibility, and sustainability, ultimately fostering better health and education outcomes.

According to the Karnataka State Education Department, over 55% of government schools lack adequate toilet facilities. The National Sanitation and Hygiene Survey 2023 further highlights the concerning state of sanitation across Indian schools:

  • Overall Access: Only 60% of schools have functional toilet facilities.
  • Gender-Specific Facilities: Just 45% of schools provide separate toilets for girls, causing higher absenteeism among female students.
  • Hygiene Standards: Nearly 70% of toilets do not meet basic hygiene standards — often lacking soap and clean water.

Target Schools: This project will prioritize underfunded government schools in low-income areas, where sanitation facilities are often inadequate or neglected.

Key objectives

  1. Improve Hygiene Standards: Renovate existing toilet facilities to meet health and hygiene standards.
  2. Enhance Accessibility: Ensure that all students, including those with disabilities, have access to adequate sanitation facilities.
  3. Promote Sustainability: Implement eco-friendly solutions, such as water-saving toilets and waste management systems.
  4. Raise Awareness: Educate students and staff about the importance of hygiene and proper sanitation practices.

Deliverable/Outcome

  1. Installation of eco friendly toilets for boys, girls and staffs for 3 to 4 schools.
  2. 1 RO Unit per school
  3. 2 to 3 toilets commodes for each school.
  4. 1 to 2 sanatory napkin burning machines for each school
  5. 3 urinals for each school
  6. Rework on Plumbing work
  7. Reworking on Flooring and painting

Qualitative Impact

  1. Improved Health: Reduction in sanitation-related illnesses among students.
  2. Enhanced Learning Environment: More students attending school with fewer absences due to health issues.
  3. Community Impact: Increased awareness of hygiene practices within the community.

Quantitative Impact

350 to 700 students & 15 to 30+ staffs will be direct beneficiaries for each year.
