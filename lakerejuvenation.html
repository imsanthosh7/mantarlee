Lake Rejuvenation

/ Lake Rejuvenation

Proposal for Lake Desilting and Rejuvenation

Project Name: Lake Rejuvenation

Location: Bangalore Rural and Surrounding

Period: 3 to 6 Months

Implementing Partner: Mantarlee Social Innovations Federation – Section 8 Company

Support Amount: ₹65 Lakhs

Project Brief

Over 60% of the country’s lakes are facing severe pollution and sedimentation issues. Key statistics include:

  • Over 20% of lakes have lost original water volume due to sedimentation.
  • 30% of freshwater fish species are at risk due to habitat loss and pollution.
  • Increased flooding risks due to reduced storage capacity in lakes, affecting surrounding communities. These challenges necessitate urgent action to protect and restore our vital water bodies.

A lake desilting project leads to sustainable and long-lasting positive change for both the environment and the community.

Key objectives

  1. Enhance Water Quality: Improve the lake's water quality by removing sediments and pollutants.
  2. Restore Biodiversity: Promote the revival of aquatic ecosystems and native species.
  3. Support Sustainable Practices: Align the project with SDGs and CSR to promote sustainable development and environmental stewardship.
  4. Strengthen Community Relations: Engage local communities in the project,fostering goodwill and collaboration.

Deliverable/Outcome

  1. Desilting & deepening the lake
  2. Removal of Solid waste in & around lake
  3. Awareness program on Lake cleanliness to village people
  4. Plantations around the lake
  5. Replicable model for other villages in Bangalore Rural

Qualitative Impact

  1. Protection and conservation of quality of water, soil and air
  2. Increase biodiversity of lake by planting native plant species
  3. Conservation of soil nutrients by the use of silt as top soil
  4. Flood mitigation by increasing storage capacity
  5. Recharges Ground water table of sounding areas/villages

Quantitative Impact

  1. Impact’s 20 to 50 villages, 5000+ farmers, 30000+ people
  2. Increase of storage capacity
  3. Impacts ground water table of 20 to 50 villages
  4. 1000+ plantations around lake bunds
  5. Attracts migratory birds and animals
  6. Increase the yield and income of farmers by the use of desilted soil
